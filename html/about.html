About me

Early Career

I started as an IT contractor picking up odd jobs on Craigslist while attending university. I worked for Geek Squad as a seasonal Field Service Implementation Engineer helping other technicians perform on-site Wi-Fi troubleshooting, performing cable runs, and TV installations.

As my contract with Geek Squad was winding down, I found a great position with Whitaker Parking Systems. During my tenure there, I worked heavily on RFID(HID) and remote computerized access control systems, ensuring PCI-DSS compliance of POS/POF devices, electro-mechanical Programmable Logic Controller (PLC) troubleshooting, CAT6 Ethernet installation, and legacy RS232 serial-to-ethernet low-voltage communications integration for VOIP(Commend) devices.

AWS

While I was an Amano-McGann Parking Systems Technician I had an opportunity to take a contract position with Amazon Web Services. I excelled in the Data Center Operations contracting role and was brought on full-time. After demonstrating my ability to quickly resolve complicated hardware tickets, Linux OS/firmware issues, document my resolutions and disseminate them via the online repo I was promoted to DCO Lead Technician/Support Engineer. This role required a variety of skills and responsibilities from technical escalation point for a team of 20 junior DCO technicians across 3 Data Centers, liaising with external vendors deploying new infrastructure, remediation of enterprise level networking equipment within tight SLA time frames, becoming the USA -East training curriculum designer for Data Center Operations in Dulles, VA., and SOP/MOP creation to limit the blast radius for external stakeholders at Fortune 500 companies.

Technologies

I worked a lot with Java at university, but currently I'm leveraging my Amazon Web Services Support Engineering background and working on projects with RHEL/CentOS, Ansible, Python, Docker, K8s, and AWS. I've been working with DevOps piplelines, Dockerizing all of my coding projects, and automating any task I can find. I've also been dabbling with Python data visualization.
