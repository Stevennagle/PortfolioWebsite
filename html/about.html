About me

Early Career

I started as an IT contractor picking up odd jobs on Craigslist while attending university. I worked for Geek Squad as a seasonal Field Service Implementation Engineer helping other technicians perform on-site Wi-Fi troubleshooting, performing cable runs, and TV installations.

As my contract with Geek Squad was winding down, I found a great position with Whitaker Parking Systems as an Amano-McGann / Skidata / Federal Parking Systems Technician. During my tenure there, I worked heavily on HID / Transcore RFID and remote computerized access control systems, ensuring PCI-DSS compliance of NCR, Ingenico, Verifone, PDI, Cybera, POS/SCO/POF devices, electro-mechanical Programmable Logic Controller (PLC) troubleshooting, CAT6 Ethernet installation, and legacy RS232 serial-to-ethernet low-voltage communications integration for VOIP(Commend) devices.

AWS

While I was a parking systems technician I had an opportunity to take a contract position with Amazon Web Services. I excelled in the Data Center Operations contracting role and was brought on full-time. After demonstrating my ability to quickly resolve complicated hardware tickets, Linux RHEL /firmware issues, document my resolutions and disseminate them via the online repo I was promoted to DCO Lead Technician. This role required a variety of skills including L1/L2/L3 fiber optic network troubleshooting and responsibilities from technical escalation point for a team of 20 junior DCO technicians across 3 Data Centers, liaising with external vendors deploying new infrastructure, remediation of enterprise level networking equipment within tight SLA time frames, becoming the USA-East training curriculum designer for Data Center Operations in Dulles, VA., overseeing the buildout of twelve 200k+ sqft datacenters, and SOP/MOP creation to limit the blast radius for external stakeholders at Fortune 500 companies.

Charter / Spectrum

I continued to build on my networking experience as a Fiber Optic Networking Specialist contractor in the Enterprise Technical Support group at Charter Communications / Spectrum. After demonstrating my ability to help large enterprise customers troubleshoot network connectivity and bandwidth utilization impacting events on multi-vendor networks, I was converted to a full-time employee as a Network Engineer in the ETS group. I currently continue to support these same customers with a more focused approach on Cisco and Juniper equipment.
